--- layout: post title: Total Lunar Eclipse date: '2010-12-20T21:51:00.000-08:00' author: Shreerang Patwardhan tags: modified_time: '2010-12-20T21:51:01.738-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-6009621700449257538.post-3467299364204354375 blogger_orig_url: http://shreerangpatwardhan.blogspot.com/2010/12/total-lunar-eclipse.html ---          On 21st December 2010 last eclipse of the year which is Total Lunar Eclipse would occur. The eclipse will be visible in the region covering Europe, West Africa, the Americas, the Pacific Ocean, eastern Australia, the Philippines and eastern and northern Asia. Whereas this eclipse is not visible in India.

Timings As Per Indian Standard Time (IST):

         Broadly speaking the Eclipse begins at 10.58 a.m. IST passing through various phases and ends at 4. 36 p.m. IST. However the Umbral phase is generally considered as actual eclipse for various ceremonies by general public. i.e. Moon enters the Umbra (dark part of earth shadow ) at 12.02 p.m IST and leaves umbra at 3.32 p.m IST. Importantly Moon enters totality phase at 1.10 p.m noon and ends at 2.24 p.m.
